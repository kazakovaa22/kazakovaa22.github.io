Photography Tips for Beginners

1. Pay Attention to Light

Try to shoot during the morning or late afternoon, when the light is soft and warm (known as the “golden hour”). Avoid harsh midday sunlight.

2. Use the Rule of Thirds

Imagine a 3×3 grid on your image. Position key subjects near the lines or their intersections to make the composition more interesting.

3. Watch the Background

A clean or blurred background helps draw focus to your subject. Avoid clutter or distractions behind your subject.

4. Don’t Be Afraid to Experiment

Try different angles, play with settings, and learn from your mistakes. The more you practice, the better you get.

5. Keep Edits Natural

Don’t over-edit your photos. Often, simplicity and natural tones create the most powerful images.
